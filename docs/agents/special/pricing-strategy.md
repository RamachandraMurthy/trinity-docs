# Pricing Strategy Agent

The Pricing Strategy Agent develops comprehensive pricing and cost strategies. It analyzes cost structures, models total cost of ownership, benchmarks against market rates, and recommends pricing approaches.

---

## What It Does

Build a defensible, competitive pricing strategy:

- **Analyze cost structure** — Understand your true costs
- **Benchmark competitively** — How does your pricing compare
- **Model TCO** — 3-year and 5-year total cost projections
- **Recommend pricing** — Value-based pricing strategies
- **Project financials** — ROI and margin analysis

---

## What You Get

**Cost Structure Breakdown:**
- Direct costs (labor, materials, licenses)
- Indirect costs (management, infrastructure)
- Risk adjustments
- Value additions and innovations

**Competitive Pricing Analysis:**
- Market rate benchmarking
- Competitor pricing patterns
- Price sensitivity assessment
- Positioning options (premium, competitive, aggressive)

**TCO Models:**
- 3-year projection with annual breakdown
- 5-year projection with annual breakdown
- Cost categories (implementation, operations, maintenance)
- Assumptions and variables

**Value-Based Pricing Recommendations:**
- Recommended price points
- Premium justification (why you can charge more)
- Discount strategies (if needed)
- Value articulation for the client

**Financial Projections:**
- Revenue and margin analysis
- ROI for the client
- Break-even analysis
- Sensitivity to key assumptions

## Input

For best results, include in your project:

- The RFP with any pricing requirements
- Your proposal response with proposed scope
- Any budget information mentioned
- Relevant past pricing (if available)

The more context about scope and requirements, the more accurate the cost modeling.

---

## Key Concepts

**Value-Based Pricing** — Pricing based on the value delivered to the client, not just your costs. The agent identifies value drivers you can emphasize.

**TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)** — The complete cost over time, including:
- Initial implementation
- Ongoing operations
- Maintenance and support
- Upgrades and enhancements
- End-of-life transition

**Premium Justification** — Why a client should pay more for your solution:
- Unique capabilities
- Lower risk
- Better outcomes
- Faster time to value

---

## Tips for Better Results

**Include your proposal** — The agent needs to understand your proposed scope to model costs accurately

**Review assumptions** — Check the TCO model assumptions against your actual cost structure

**Consider scenarios** — Use the sensitivity analysis to understand how pricing might flex

**Align with strategy** — Decide if you're positioning as premium, competitive, or aggressive
